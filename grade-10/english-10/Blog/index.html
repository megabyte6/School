When I first started programming, I had no idea what I was doing. I was only ten or eleven at the time so, to be honest, I did not know what to expect. I had just dipped my toes into this vast world and played around with all sorts of things without really having a path. I soon realized that I would need to choose a project to work on if I actually wanted to learn how to code. So I made my decision to just give it a try. One of my earliest projects was a simple website of Julius Caesar.

Over time, I learned other things, new programming languages, and more about how computers work. Some things got pretty boring, but I knew that there was always more to be learned so I never lost my hunger for new knowledge.
